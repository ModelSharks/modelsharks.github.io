Estimating Arizona

By the Model Sharks

Our model's purpose

The purpose of our model is to estimate the number of homes within the Scottsdale city limits.

Background Information

Incorporated in 1951, Scottsdale is located in the Phoenix metro area, in Arizona. Like most areas in Arizona, Scottsdale is a desert, with an average elevation of 1,818 feet.

The average home size in Scottsdale is 1,700 square-feet with an average lot size of 8,726 square feet in its state and an average housing density of 756.7 houses per square mile, with 51% of homes being residential homes.

The total area of Scottsdale is 184.5 square miles , stretching 31 miles from north to south.

Terminology

To ensure our model is as accurate as possible, we made definite terms to reduce ambiguity:

  • Homes are single-family homes, apartments, and townhomes.
  • Scottsdale, or the Scottsdale area, is the Scottsdale city limits.
  • Homes are completed homes, not ones which are under construction.

Model

We used many variables in our first attempt at an algorithm:

  • L - Square miles of land (184.5 mi2)
  • R - Percentage of residential homes (51%)
  • F - Percent of single-family homes (60.45%)
  • S - Average lot size (8,726 feet2)
  • H - Number of homes (what we are calculating)
  • D - Housing density (756.7 mi2)

In our initial attempt, we tried using the following formula:

H = (L * R * F) / S

This gave us a number well-below one (around 0.006), so we attempted a new formula instead:

H = L * R * D

Which gave us a much more promising number of 71,202 homes.
